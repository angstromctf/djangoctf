{% endblock %} {% block content %} Welcome to angstromCTF, a new capture-the-flag (CTF) competition hosted by high school students! CTF cybersecurity competitions have become an increasingly popular form of challenges for aspiring cybersecurity students. These competitions are designed to educate and inspire high school students through interactive hacking challenges. After the success of high-school CTF games (especially picoCTF), we decided to run our own CTF competition called angstromCTF. angstromCTF will consist of questions ranging from trivial to extremely difficult in various categories:

  • Binary exploitation
  • Reverse engineering
  • Cryptography
  • Web exploitation
  • Forensics
  • Hardware
  • Combination
angstromCTF will be similar to many high other school CTFs that we've participated in:

  • picoCTF
  • sCTF
  • HSCTF
  • CAMSCTF
  • TJCTF
However, unlike many other high school CTF competitions, angstromCTF will focus less on forensics and have no reconnaisance problems (i.e. problems where you need to hunt down hidden information across the internet). It will instead focus more on cryptography and binary exploitation, areas we feel have been underrepresented in other high-school CTFs. {% endblock %}

Welcome to angstromCTF, a new capture-the-flag (CTF) competition hosted and written by high school students! CTF cybersecurity competitions have become an increasingly popular form of challenges for aspiring cybersecurity students. These competitions are designed to educate and inspire high school students through interactive hacking challenges. After we saw the great success of high-school CTF games (especially picoCTF), we decided to run our own CTF competition called angstromCTF.

Students will compete in teams of up to five members. Points are given for answering each question (entering the flag). Prizes will be awarded to the top-scoring US high-school teams. angstromCTF is meant for both beginners and experienced CTFers and consists of questions ranging from simple to extremely difficult in various categories. If you have any questions, join our IRC here or #angstromctf on freenode.


Cryptography

Crack codes and ciphers, ranging from the simple (Caesar cipher, Vigenère cipher) to the complex (RSA, elliptic curves). Do cryptanalysis, use deduction, and get the flags!

Binary Exploitation

Apply a range of skills to exploit security vulnerabilities in programs, including buffer overflows, arithmetic overflows, format string bugs, and use-after-frees. Force these binaries to run your own code and read the flag!

Web Exploitation

Find various vulnerabilities in web pages, and use them to get access to areas you shouldn't. This topic covers a wide range of vulnerabilities, including SQL injection and mistakes in using scripting languages. Use your new-found access to recover the flag!

Reverse Engineering

Examine programs given to you and find out their code, from simple Java apps to more difficult ELF binaries. Figure out their inner workings and use the information you gather to find the flag!

Forensics

Explore the art of digital forensics, which is all about uncovering hidden data within a large swath of information. Solve challenging puzzles in areas such as stenography, memory forensics, data recovery, and system examination. You can find the flag if you look carefully enough!
